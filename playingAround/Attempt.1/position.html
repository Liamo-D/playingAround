Preperations

Although the Bunny has displayed no outward signs of aggression so far and is seemingly neutral in all current ongoing conflicts, it is an oversight to not prepare for the worst situation, a change of his attitude. If such a situation were to occur, we as a species need to have clauses in place to deal with him.

Daffy

Our primary contingency plan at this current period of time is to manipulate Daffy into going against his long-time partner. He is the only creature that has a potential equality to Bugs's power. Although unlikely that the two would turn on each other, Daffy has shown himself to be self-centred and short-sighted. It may be possible to convince him through incentives and false promises. If this plan were to be successful, however, it wouldleave us again unprepared for the event that Daffy would in turn begin to get aggresive towards humanity. This plan requires the two to destroy or immobilise each other, neither of which can be relied on. This plan has the best chance of successfully taking down Bugs, but the variables contained make it an unreliable defence.

Firepower

The secondary contingency is to hit the Bunny with every ounce of explosives available to us. Unfortunately, the calculated explosive power required to eliminate a being of his calibre would also result in the end of all biological life on this planet. If such a threat occured that it became neccesary to use this last-resort fallback, an evaucation plan has been developed in the hopes of keeping humanity alive. The United Nations has brokered a treatywith all nuke-capable and space-fairing nations, that under the dire threat of the Bugs once the signal has been made, within two days 80,000 people from all corners of the Earth will be evacuated into space, along with samples of flora and fauna that are alreadykept on an emergency space station, and all capable countries will direct their power onto Bugs's location. It is a suicide call for everyone involved, but it could be humanities only chance at survival.

Isolation

Unlike the other contingency plans, this does not lead to a direct and indefensible threat to humanity. It is a simple idea with a difficult execution, but should the plan be accurately and effectively completed it may leave us with the tools to safely dispose of the Bunny as a threat. The solution is to temporarily apprehend the Bunny, long enough to equip him onto a rocket and send him into orbit and beyond. It is not a permanent fix by any means. Research has proven that he can survive a long, seemingly indefinite time without oxygen. It is also assumed that the vaccum and temperature of space does not affect him. It will not leave us free from the threat and danger he poses, but it will leave us with crucial and valuable time. Global funds and resources will be pooled together in an emergency task force, in the hopes that technology and understanding advancements will leave us in a preferential position should he ever find a way to return to his home.

Last Statement

We are developing these plans only because compared to Bugs, we are weak and stupid. His knowledge and wisdom far surpasses our own understanding of our world and the greater universe beyond. It is a far-fetched idea that he would become aggressive to humans, after spending so many hours walking with them through the millennia and cultivating their knowledge and ideas by leaps and bounds. His ablilities would give him awareness of any contingcy plans made for him before they have left the lips of whoever is speaking, and that is truly terrifying. Compared to him, humanity is powerless.
